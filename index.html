Drone view
The drone must track the target height while the plant changes. Battery fade reduces thrust authority via k(t), and at 15 s the payload is dropped so m(t) decreases by 30%.

Model
The simulated drone follows x¨(t) = k(t)u(t) - m(t)g with state x = height. A good feedforward guess is roughly u_ff ≈ m_nom g / k_nom, and PID then corrects the remaining error. The score penalizes tracking error, control effort, and large control moves.
Teaching tips
Start in manual mode so students feel how difficult it is to reject plant changes. Then switch to PID + feedforward and discuss why feedforward removes most of the steady thrust demand, while integral action compensates for battery fade and the mass drop.
